Yayride

UX case study for a mobile application that empowers people mobility

Overview

Yayride is a mobile ride-hailing application that offers vechicle for hire. Yayride aims to reshapen Nigeria's rogue system of transportation, powering public mobility, allow riders connect to local drivers to reach their destination by providing access to an intergrated network of the world's most advance public means of transportation option through a single, digital interface.

Challenges

  1. The mobile application aims to grow passengers base fast.
  2. Create a comfortable platform with familiar interface that local rides can easily interact with.
  3. Provide a product that assures users that their safty is prioritize, giving them a walkthrough and transparent view through the app features on their ride details and assigned driver's credentials.
  4. Create accessable payment method option

User Research

During the user research process, I take alot of things into account such as user behaviour and pain points. I make use of Surveys and interviews to gather data as it aids me to curate my approach to solving the project challenges.
User research are a core user experience method, That would bring enough data and inputs to generate concepts and hypothesis. Below I highlighted the user research processes I used;

Survey


A survey was conducted with frequent yayaride or similar product users with the use of google form. The questions were based on habits, motivation, and challenges while using a ride-hailing applications, The responses were collected in the form of multiple choice and short answers to get more insights at an early stage.

Interview


The interview sample took into account men and women aged from 18 to 45, who are frequent users of ride-hailing services of the city of Lagos, Nigeria, with different mobility habits. The interview took approximately 10-15 minutes and included topics to get to the core of what users are trying to do and what their problems are. So I asked:
  1. What motivates you to using ride-hailing app, rather than a traditional taxi?
  2. How often do you use the service?
  3. Can you tell me a little bit about your mobility habits?
  4. If there's one the you could change about ride-hailing application, what would that be?
  5. What are the challenges you face when using these mobile app services?

User Testing

I recruited 5 people to test the prototype. The participants were aged between 25 - 50yrs a regular yayride and similar product users.
  1. How easily users are able to set up the app and account
  2. How easily users are able to set their drop off, edit their pickup point
  3. How easily users are able to request a ride seamlessly
  4. How easily users are able to use the feedback feature
  5. How does the overall experience feel like
  6. What improvements need to be made

Validation

After conducting user testing, The results were better, users found it easy to request a ride, View their driver's credentials and verfication, moreover they thought the feedback feature came in handy as they can send their feedback to yayride through "texting", "sending a photo" with the use of a camera feature and as well "voice recording" they can send a voice note, which can add their experience with customer services.
The use of the microphone feature aids accessability experience, users with disabilities such as blindness, can enable the talk back feature located in the menu screen and as well us the microphone feature apply their drop off or pick-up point.
They found the interface familiar and easy to navigate around. which was the core back of the testing phase. I'd say I was satified with the results, also the use of usability tests, and other in-depth surveys with the user to improve the initial version.

Take Away

More usability testing, Research, and Analytics would uncover additional pain points and would lead us to make better design decisions. One takeaway from working on this project was understanding the value of user research and planning to synthesize findings in the most helpful way to our users within our constraints.
